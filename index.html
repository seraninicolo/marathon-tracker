How your score is calculated

The idea

The leaderboard does not reward who runs the most. It rewards who follows their own plan most consistently. Someone doing 30km/week perfectly beats someone doing 80km/week sloppily.

Step 1 — Session compliance score

Every time you log a completed workout, it is compared to what you planned for that day.

Compliance = (actual km ÷ planned km) × 100, capped at 100
Planned 10km, ran 10.5km100 pts Done
Planned 10km, ran 8km80 pts Partial
Planned 10km, ran 6km60 pts Partial
Session completely missed0 pts Missed
No planned session, logged anyway100 pts Done

Sessions below 70% compliance are marked as Partial. Sessions you skip entirely are Missed.

Step 2 — Overall score

Your leaderboard score combines two things:

Score = (avg compliance × 60%) + (sessions completed rate × 40%)
Average compliance60% weight
Sessions completed ÷ sessions due40% weight

Example: you completed 8 of 10 sessions (80%) with an average compliance of 95%. Score = (95 × 0.6) + (80 × 0.4) = 89.

Goal prediction

The green or red pill under your name on the leaderboard estimates if your current training volume is sufficient to hit your target time. It looks at your recent weekly km and longest run, then compares them to benchmarks for your goal pace.

Sub 3:3070+ km/w, 32km long run
Sub 3:4555+ km/w, 28km long run
Sub 4:0045+ km/w, 25km long run
Sub 4:3035+ km/w, 22km long run

The prediction updates automatically every time you log a run. 15% tolerance is applied — you do not need to hit the exact number.

Race view

The Race tab shows everyone as animated runners on a track. Position left to right reflects your current score. The gap between runners is proportional — a 10-point lead looks like a 10-point lead.
